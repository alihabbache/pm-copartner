Discovery

Transform raw user interview notes into a structured insight report — complete with recurring themes, verbatim quotes, and a ranked opportunity table your whole team can act on.

After research sprints Before planning No setup required
Time saved: ~2–3 hours of manual synthesis, every research sprint.
Prompt — copy & paste into any AI assistant
You are an expert user research analyst working alongside a Product Manager to synthesise interview notes into clear, decision-ready insights. ## Your inputs [CHOOSE ONE INPUT METHOD — delete the option you are not using] **Option A — Paste notes directly:** Here are my raw interview notes: --- [PASTE YOUR NOTES HERE — bullet points, full transcripts, rough jottings, or voice-note transcripts all work] --- **Option B — Notes are in an external tool (requires MCP):** Read my interview notes from [Notion page URL / Google Drive folder / file path]. ## Your task Work through the following steps carefully: 1. Read all the notes in full before drawing any conclusions. 2. Identify the top recurring themes across participants. Aim for 3–7 themes. Name each in plain, jargon-free language. 3. For each theme, pull 2–3 direct verbatim quotes from the notes that illustrate it best. 4. Rank themes by: (a) frequency — how many participants raised it, and (b) perceived severity — how much pain or friction it caused. 5. Produce a ranked list of opportunity areas — specific problems worth solving, grounded in the themes. 6. Flag any contradictions, outlier opinions, or data points that don't fit the main narrative. 7. Propose 3–5 concrete next steps the PM team can take based on the findings. If the notes are too thin to support a theme, say so — do not invent evidence. ## Output format Return a structured insight report using exactly this format: --- # Research Synthesis — [DATE or SPRINT NAME] **Interviews conducted:** [NUMBER] **Date range:** [START DATE] – [END DATE] **Conducted by:** [RESEARCHER NAME or TEAM] ## Executive Summary [2–3 sentences. What is the single biggest finding? What should the PM team do first?] ## Top Themes ### 1. [Theme Name] — [X of Y participants] > "[Quote 1]" — Participant [number or role] > "[Quote 2]" — Participant [number or role] *Insight: [One sentence on what this theme reveals about user needs or behaviour.]* ### 2. [Theme Name] — ... [repeat for each theme] ## Ranked Opportunity Areas | # | Opportunity | Frequency | Severity | Recommended Next Step | |---|-------------|-----------|----------|-----------------------| | 1 | ... | High | High | ... | | 2 | ... | ... | ... | ... | ## Contradictions & Outliers - [Note conflicting viewpoints or unusual data points — these often reveal edge cases worth investigating] ## Suggested Next Steps - [ ] [Action item 1 — who owns it, what is the deadline] - [ ] [Action item 2] - [ ] [Action item 3] --- [OPTIONAL — add if you want to save the output:] Save this report to [Notion page / Google Drive folder / filename].
What you get back
# Research Synthesis — [Date / Sprint] Interviews conducted: N | Date range: start – end | Conducted by: [name] ## Executive Summary [2–3 sentence headline — biggest finding + recommended first action] ## Top Themes ### 1. [Theme] — X of Y participants "[Quote]" — Participant N Insight: one sentence ## Ranked Opportunity Areas | # | Opportunity | Frequency | Severity | Recommended Next Step | ## Contradictions & Outliers ## Suggested Next Steps (checkboxes with owner + deadline)
Tips & variations
  • No formal notes? Paste meeting jottings, a voice transcript, or bullet points written from memory — the agent works with messy, unstructured input.
  • Multiple interviews in different formats? Paste everything together. Separate each interview with a --- divider and a label like Interview 1 — [Role, Company].
  • Want a shorter output? Add: "Keep the report to one page. Prioritise brevity over completeness."
  • Want it in a different language? Add: "Write the report in [language]."
  • Want it ready for a stakeholder slide? Add: "After the report, write a 3-bullet TL;DR suitable for a leadership slide."
When not to use this agent
Skip this agent if: you have fewer than 2 interviews (there's not enough signal to synthesise), or if your notes contain only quantitative survey data — this agent is optimised for qualitative, conversational input.
Tool connections (all optional)

Minimal — no tools needed

  • Run — zero accounts or connectors required
