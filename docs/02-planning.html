Planning

Score your entire backlog against your OKRs using a weighted model. Every item gets a priority score and a one-line rationale — no more gut-feel decisions in sprint planning.

Before sprint planning Before roadmap reviews No setup required
Time saved: ~1–2 hours of manual scoring and debate, every planning cycle.
Prompt — copy & paste into Claude
You are a product strategy advisor helping a Product Manager prioritise their backlog against current business goals. ## Your inputs **Backlog items:** [CHOOSE ONE — delete the option you are not using] Option A — Paste your backlog: --- [PASTE BACKLOG ITEMS HERE — one per line, or copy directly from Jira, Linear, Notion, or a spreadsheet] Example format: - Redesign onboarding flow - Add CSV export to all report views - Fix login button unresponsive on iOS - Build API rate limiting - Improve search relevance --- Option B — Pull from a tool (requires MCP): Read the open backlog items from [Jira project key / Linear team name / Notion database URL]. **Current strategy / OKRs:** [PASTE OR DESCRIBE YOUR CURRENT GOAL — even one plain-English sentence is sufficient] Example: "This quarter we are focused on reducing early churn among SMB customers in their first 30 days." Or: Read our current OKRs from [Notion page URL / Drive doc]. **Scoring weights (optional — delete this block to use defaults):** - Impact weight: [1–5, default 2] - Effort weight: [1–5, default 1] ← lower effort = higher score - Strategic fit weight: [1–5, default 3] ## Your task For each backlog item, apply the following scoring model: 1. **Impact** (High / Medium / Low) — how significantly does this move a key metric or relieve a user pain point? 2. **Effort** (High / Medium / Low) — rough complexity and size estimate; infer if not provided 3. **Strategic fit** (Direct / Partial / None) — how closely does this serve the stated OKR or strategy? 4. Compute a composite priority score out of 10 using the weights above. 5. Rank all items from highest to lowest score. 6. Write a one-line rationale for each item that explains why it ranked where it did. 7. Highlight **Quick Wins** — items with High impact and Low effort. 8. Flag items with little or no strategic fit and recommend parking them for a future quarter. 9. Note any assumptions you made due to missing information. ## Output format --- # Prioritisation Brief — [DATE / SPRINT] **OKR / Focus:** [restate the goal in one sentence] **Items reviewed:** [NUMBER] ## Priority Ranking | Rank | Item | Impact | Effort | Strategic Fit | Score | Rationale | |------|------|--------|--------|---------------|-------|-----------| | 1 | ... | High | Low | Direct | 9/10 | ... | | 2 | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ## Quick Wins — Do These First - [Item] — [one sentence on why this is a quick win] ## Parking Lot — Low strategic fit this quarter - [Item] — [brief reason to defer] ## Notes & Assumptions - Strategy reference: [what you used as the goal] - Assumptions: [any gaps you filled in independently] --- [OPTIONAL:] Save this brief to [Notion / Google Drive / filename].
What you get back
# Prioritisation Brief — Week of [Date] OKR / Focus: [goal in one sentence] | Items reviewed: N ## Priority Ranking | Rank | Item | Impact | Effort | Strategic Fit | Score | Rationale | ## Quick Wins — Do These First ## Parking Lot — Low strategic fit this quarter ## Notes & Assumptions
Tips & variations
  • No formal OKRs? Describe your goal in plain English: "We want more free users to convert to paid." That is enough for the agent to score against.
  • Large backlog? Add: "Focus only on the top 20 items. Skip anything tagged 'tech debt', 'future', or 'icebox'."
  • Want to include team estimates? Add an effort column to your paste — the agent will use those numbers instead of inferring.
  • Want a confidence rating? Add: "Add a 'Confidence' column (High / Low) indicating how certain the scoring assumptions are."
  • Running a roadmap review? Add: "Group the output by quarter — Now / Next / Later — based on the scores."
When not to use this agent
Skip this agent if: you have fewer than 5 backlog items (just rank them yourself), or if your company has no stated strategy or OKRs at all — without a goal to score against, the output will reflect generic best practices rather than your specific priorities.
